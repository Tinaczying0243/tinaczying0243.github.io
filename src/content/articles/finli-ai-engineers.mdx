Finli Inc., the seven-year-old San Marino, California-based fintech startup, expects revenue to climb nearly fivefold to about $30 million this year after cutting its engineering team to two from 15 people as AI tools helped lower costs.

Artificial-intelligence tools have reduced Finli's monthly cost by about 80%, the company said, allowing it to operate with a much leaner team. The firm's software lets small businesses manage invoices, quotes and recurring payments through their banks, replacing the 10 to 12 tools many use to run daily operations.

Finli is also building an AI-powered sales assistant for bankers, designed to replace junior staff who typically turn over within 18 months and can cost about $100,000 a year, the company said, reflecting salaries and turnover typical for junior sales roles. The tool, which can be deployed for about $500 a month, helps bankers prepare for client meetings and pitch products.

"If I were starting today, I would take $0 from venture capital," Chief Executive Officer Lori Shao said. "The most valuable thing is distribution." The shift toward AI-driven development has reshaped how the company manages costs and growth, allowing it to prioritize larger contracts and sustain profit margins of at least 30%.

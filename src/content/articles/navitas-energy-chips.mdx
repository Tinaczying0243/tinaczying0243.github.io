Navitas Semiconductor Corp. (NVTS) rose 21% on March 11 after launching two new products aimed at AI data centers and energy & grid infrastructure. Shares of the power semiconductor company climbed to $10.52, their highest close since Jan. 22, making it the top performer among its peers.

The Torrance, California-based chip designer uses materials like gallium nitride to make chips that can operate at higher temperatures and voltages in smaller sizes than traditional silicon chips. The new packages improve heat dissipation while using less space in high-power systems such as AI data center power supplies, electronic vehicles markets, and industrial electrification.

The company's early customers include consumer electronics makers such as Samsung and Xiaomi, according to Bloomberg supply-chain data. It is shifting its focus from consumer markets to higher-power markets including customers like Amazon. In the last quarter, revenue from mobile markets fell to below 25% for the first time.

The rapid expansion of artificial intelligence and data centers is boosting electricity demand. By 2035, data centers may consume about 1,600 terawatt-hours, roughly 4.4% of global electricity, according to BloombergNEF. The trend is pushing the company to realign resources toward high-power markets, Chief Executive Officer Chris Allexandre said. "I've seen numerous proof points that the new technology adoption is accelerating. AI is a catalyst, changing the game across markets."
